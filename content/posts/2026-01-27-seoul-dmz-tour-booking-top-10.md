## Before You Go: ID Rules, Dress Code, Photo Restrictions, and Seasonal Packing

This is the section that prevents the most disappointment. The DMZ area is sensitive, and rules are enforced.

### ID rules (non-negotiable)
Bring valid identification:
- **Passport** (recommended for international travelers)
- Korean residents: accepted local ID per tour requirements

If you forget your ID, you may be denied participation even if you paid. Treat ID as essential as your phone.

### Dress code: keep it neat and practical
While exact restrictions can vary by access area and current regulations, the safest approach is:
- Closed-toe shoes (the tunnel is the main reason)  
- Avoid overly revealing outfits  
- Avoid sloppy footwear (like flimsy slippers) that can be unsafe

Comfort matters, but so does respecting the site‚Äôs military-adjacent setting.

### Photo restrictions: follow your guide
Some areas allow photos; others don‚Äôt. Rules can change, and enforcement can be strict. The best strategy:
- Ask your guide where photography is allowed  
- Don‚Äôt assume ‚Äúno sign = yes‚Äù  
- Avoid filming soldiers or restricted infrastructure

If you‚Äôre a serious photographer, consider a small-group or private option where you can ask more questions about where/when to shoot‚Äîwhile still following all restrictions.

### Seasonal packing (Seoul departure tours)
- **Spring / Fall**: layers (mornings can be cool), and consider sunglasses for observatory glare. These seasons often bring clearer skies, improving viewing conditions.  
- **Summer**: light clothing plus water; humidity can be intense. A small towel and portable fan can help.  
- **Winter**: serious warmth (gloves, hat, thermal layer). Wind can cut through at outdoor viewpoints. Snow can also cause schedule changes.

Always pack:
- ID  
- water  
- comfortable walking shoes  
- a light layer (even in warmer months, the tunnel can feel cool)
